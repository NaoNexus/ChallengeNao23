Our project for the NaoChallenge 2023 revolves around the theme of eco-living, living ecologically. We chose this direction because we firmly believe in its actuality, considering the hot topic of climate change. This year we are supported by Amperia, an important company in the industrial sector of energetic eco-sustainability with ramifications throughout Italy. Their main objective is to increase the efficiency and reduce the emissions of all types of structures through improvements to their electrical systems. Their expertise has allowed us to widen our reach, with the goal of achieving new feats in a technological field destined to grow exponentially in the future.

For this NaoChallenge, the NaoNexus Team developed an interactive system aimed at analysing to the finest detail the energy consumption of a client and, subsequently, propose modifications that would render their structure more eco-friendly. Our plan is to make it applicable to any context, with a focus on schools and other academic institutions.

NAO in our I.C.T. lab

NAO at Amperia's headquarters

Nao is at the fulcrum of this process. The humanoid robot analyses data with its numerous sensors, capable of detecting even the slightest variations in carbon dioxide, humidity and brightness levels. It uses the retrieved data to offer smart suggestions aimed at improving the efficiency of the studied system. Thanks to a different sensor kindly donated by Amperia, our metallic friend is also capable of calculating particular statistics, such as the exact number of trees to plant to minimize one's carbon footprint.

NAO's analysis is then communicated to a potential user in the form of a dialogue with the robot itself. It is capable of answering any type of question and give tips on possible actions that would improve the client's situation. For example, NAO is able to specify the exact number of photovoltaic panels required to reduce to zero one's emissions.

Meeting with Amperia

On March 24th 2023, the NaoNexus team traveled to Amperia's offices to discuss on the project. They were welcomed by Massimo Polato, CEO of the company. He helped us refine our work and provided relevant insights on clean energy production and consumption.

The key points discussed were experimentation, data collection and raising awareness. The NaoNexus team and Amperia agree that, while the first two are undoubtedly important, especially on technical side of things, they are subordinate to the third. We both believe that in order to fix a problem, one must first understand it. Being familiar with one’s individual carbon footprint is necessary to find suitable solutions intended to reduce it. From solar communities to new technologies aimed at producing and storing clean energy, the team left the meeting a new, enriched perspective.
